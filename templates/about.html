I first went on stage in my mother’s womb. As a child in a family of dancers, I yearned for the black marley and the hot incandescent lights. Every idea was worthy of expression through dance.

If we meet, chances are you will probably remark that I’m very tall. On aerial straps, I present an unusual combination of extraordinarily long limbs and momentum based acrobatics. To me performance is a celebration, and the movements I have cultivated on straps derive from the pure joy of flight. I delight in sharing this joy with my audiences. I also use my creative work to express ferocity, and to explore lesser known, bold and strong aspects of femininity.

I learned straps and acrobatics under the tutelage of many phenomenal coaches to whom I am eternally grateful, but the bulk of my training was with Chloe Farah and Victor Fomine in Montreal.

From Las Vegas to Macau, I have performed in shows around the world in styles varying from circus tent to cabaret to 20-meter-high arena. I also have done work on aerial rope, fabric, and as an ensemble dancer.
